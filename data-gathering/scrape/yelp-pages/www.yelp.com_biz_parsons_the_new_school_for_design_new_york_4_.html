      Review from Savannah F.

       

      14friends

      5reviews

      Savannah F.

      Los Angeles, CA

      5/26/2010

      Got me to leave South Carolina. I go to Parsons not for fashion but for Interior Design, however I have some friends in fashion-like many have said-it's a love/hate relationship. If you can survive the 4 years you will most likely get a great job at the end of it.

      Keep in mind that you will work your ass off, no life outside school, all nighters are a norm., and most likely you will not have the time to enjoy the city as much as you thought you were going to. Also straight, good looking men do NOT go to this school period-I guess that's not a surprise haha.

      However, you will  meet some amazing contacts and get some real world experiences, and access to resources that you wouldn't have otherwise. Also many of the internships through the school are fashion related so that's always a great opportunity if your going into that sort of thing.Also the garment district in NY def. owns L.A-the variety of different fabrics is amazing!

      Additionally, I wish someone would have told me before I transferred to Parsons to prepare yourself as much as you can before you start school-for me that entails drafting, model making,autocad,rhino, ect..For fashion I would assume sewing, draping, mastering how to draw croquis, familiarity with textiles and fabrics, ect.. Good luck!

      Review from Lizz K.

       

      21friends

      60reviews

      Lizz K.

      Brooklyn, NY

      4/21/2007

      Parsons. Where else can you pay $40k a year to go and spend nearly every waking hour of your day for four whole years in a building with no climate control, security guards who harass you and call you stupid and scary unisex bathrooms with no lights and wild west swinging saloon style doors that don't lock? To be fair, I'm not a fashion student, so it's very possible that their big fancy building way up on 7th ave has like, you know, sinks that work in their bathrooms, but I wouldn't know.

      Actually, I love this school and I'm glad that I go here..but I am kind of bummed out over the way the New School is taking over and mucking things up. They've also started letting way too many people in since all the Project Runway business (Tim Gunn doesn't even work here anymore, dudes), which is especially a downer for smaller departments (like photography) that already have such limited space and facilities as is.

      Lastly, please don't judge us by the hideous new logo that the New School people forced upon us. We all hate it too and are embarrassed by the fact that a supposedly top level design school is being represented by a circa 1992 design clearly chosen by some out of touch dude in a suit.

      Review from Dennie C.

       

      24friends

      102reviews

      Dennie C.

      Yonkers, NY

      9/12/2007

      I finished my design studies at Parson about a year and a half ago and aside from the ugly building and poor facilities I've found the instruction itself to be hit or miss.  The hits came from professors who were well experienced in the field who had a lot of professional and personal insight into the various classes they covered.  The misses mostly came from recent graduate students who had little experience in their field and also little expertise in teaching.  Overall I had more professors in the hits category but there were some real disappointments.

      The other students were interesting though and came from all kinds of backgrounds and although this is a somewhat expensive school its not filled with rich brats as you might find in other pricey NY schools.

      Review from Jen W.

      Elite '11

      75friends

      345reviews

      Jen W.

      Flushing, NY

      11/19/2007

      If you want to waste about 60G and 2 years of your life on a masters program than this is the place for you! If you want to be tossed into the mass of students and be over looked then you should go here. If you want to be constantly harassed by security guards who obviously didn't finish their schooling and ending up hating students that actually got into school and can go then you should go here. I for one am not into this masochistic behavior and did not enjoy my time spent there. I got my masters there and sometimes I wonder why I just didn't take some continuing education classes instead.
      My dept especially was filled with design failures that ended up teaching and hated it. It was filled with a politics and high school behavior amongst the faculty. Those who were promoted to director positions did not deserve it nor were able to handle the pressure nor the responsibilities of it. Unprofessional behavior was rampant there. There was no budget to do anything because apparently it was spent on other useless things that 1 particular student wanted or needed. There was favoritism, a few select students were chosen and the rest of the students were left to fend for themselves. Not very friendly. You are left to fend for yourself, find ways to get supplies and do your own projects. Forces you to be resourceful, but critics were sometime useless and made personal attacks at students rather than the actual work.
      I feel that the undergrad program was better than the masters program. Not worth it. Fake, backstabbing and unprofessional. And the president of the school has political ties that he brought to graduation. Students were NOT amused.
