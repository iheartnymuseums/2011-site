      Review from Svetlana Y.

       

      0friends

      6reviews

      Svetlana Y.

      Brooklyn, NY

      11/20/2010

      Everyone who is writing the super negative reviews here must not have ever attended another college. I have recently graduated from a very well known private art college in NY, so I know what I'm talking about.

      One important thing, and I can't stress this enough - If you are planning to major in anything Other Than Design (fine art, photography, illustration etc.) - do not go here, it will suck. All designers proceed reading the review.

      I decided to go to FIT right after graduating with a bachelor's degree because I wanted a change in my career plans. Even though my original major and college experience provided me with amazing education, I wanted to pursue accessories design and FIT was perfect because:

      - it's a 2 and 2 year college, so I could get another bachelor's degree in just 2 years.
      - the level of education I am receiving is comparable to what I got before at just a fraction of the cost.
      - most of the professors are extremely helpful and will go out of their way to assist you.
      - the career services office actually does a good job helping students.
      - they accepted my AP credits from 5 years go. My scores were good, but not good enough to get credit in private schools.
      - the computer center is open almost 24/7

      negative:
      - yes, the offices are a pain and do sometimes make mistakes. just smile and nod
      - a lot of the students are not motivated enough. The "good enough" attitude is sometimes a downer. You need to really be on top of things and push yourself. If you want to do more work or learn something extra, just ask the professors and they will help you with your projects. The kind of pressure I felt at my old school is just not present here.
      - not many critiques/ discussions of student projects

      In the end:
      FIT is more of a vocational school. It will teach you technique and the know how for creating things. It will not teach you about the design process, you will never be asked to justify what you made beyond "it's pretty and I felt like it and it's well crafted." I think that this is an excellent place for someone who already received another type of education and is looking more for technical skill and a lot of the students in the BA program are older than the typical 20-21 you'd expect. FIT specializes more in making not thinking.

      As for the people who gave extremely negative reviews, Guess what - most colleges have awful staff working in the offices and lose a lot of paper work and make you jump through hoops to get even the simplest things like registering for classes done. No matter where you go, not all of your professors are going to be magical and great. You need to take everything with a grain of salt. The cafeteria food at my old school sucked too (I am very jealous of colleges with hood food in their dining halls, but there are very few of them), but you're surrounded by so many restaurants in the city (many inexpensive ones), just pick one. I'm sorry that you got laid off from your job, but that's something called economy and has nothing to do with the school, you're just being bitter. To the person who reviewed the museum - it's a good Free museum in a school, just be glad that such a thing exists, if you want to be entertained for hours - go to the met.

      Brooklyn, NY

      10/15/2007

      Oh, Fashion Institute, how I despise thee! Where do I begin?

      The only reason FIT gets even 2 stars is because of the first semester I was there... however it was more the city and some of the people that interested me and kept me connected (oh, and the fact that I was one of the only 5 straight guys in school that is drowning in pretty girls who get free drinks and willingly accept any man into their dorm rooms). This was my first time in NYC.

      The classes at the beginning were intriguing enough, but I was so naive. They got worse. Much worse. The photography department showed signs of being rather archaic and out of touch with the digital world. The Liberal arts were... well... heh.

      I attempted to dam up an ongoing flood of intense Bush-hating, so-open-minded-my-brains-are-spilling-out liberalism by establishing a countering voice via a student group on campus but if you have any conservatism in your soul you will likely find the school's departments are filled with faculty who are less concerned about your rights on campus and more concerned about brainwashing you in a basic math or art history course with non-related socio-political matters (That's breaking your "academic freedom" rights by the way).

      The food in the cafeteria is rather grotesque and overpriced. Lackluster workmanship plagues the dining hall and it's unfortunate the employees can't be fired for inefficiency because of their solid lets-keep-bad-employees-hired-regardless-of-work-e thic union. But you're not coming for the school for the food, right? Well, unless you plan on dorming there because you must purchase an option in their meal system.

      The people will wear on you. The sex-on-the-beach drinking, stick-it-in-me-now sexually depraved, intellectually vapid girls will cause even the most chauvinistic men to turn their heads in time.
      You'll begin to wonder why certain professors are even still on the staff because they, too, like certain cafeteria workers, are not the greatest in their field. In fact, if you replace half of the professors with the cafeteria employees you'd see no diminished quality in either environments.

      And don't be concerned about the seemingly unnecessary tuition and arbitrary "technology" and miscellaneous fees. It's all worth it, because without some fees here and there how would the school be able to organize its annual, student-funded drag queen show, complete with LGBT figureheads like Amanda Lepore? If you don't know who she is, go to FIT and you'll know soon enough. The FIT community idolizes her.

      If you're looking for intellectual stimulation, time worth spending in brainstorming (beyond pondering how to leave the school), substantive debates, individuality (no, the cloned girls walking around in their sweatpants with "PINK" plastered on their asses do not count as being an "individual"), a fantastic staff and student association, an efficient and helpful bureaucracy dedicated to the well-being and improvement of students**, a financial aid department that doesn't treat you like shit, and security guards that speak fluent English.... LOOK ELSEWHERE!

      The good parts? Hetero- and homosexual men will enjoy themselves in their first semester.

      But don't ever rely on the escalators to work properly if you have classes on the 8th floor. Sneaking into the staff-only elevator is your best bet if you're running late.

      ** Don't even get me started on this topic. It's like pulling teeth if you have any kind of problem that needs a rather immediate solution, or even any long-term solution. Love FIT or hate it, all students will agree with this.
