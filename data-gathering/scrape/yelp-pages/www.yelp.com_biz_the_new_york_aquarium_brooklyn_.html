      Review from Amanda H.

      Amanda H.

      New York, NY

      10/11/2010 2 photos

      It's now been just over a year since I visited the NY Aquarium accompanied by an amazing 5 year old.  We started our adventure at the Sea Lion/Otter show, which was cool.  The show was run very professionally, the animals did some tricks, water jumps etc.  We went around to all of the different displays and saw sharks, fish, sea animals, creatures - it was educational, beautiful, and entertaining.  

      Overall the place seemed a little run down compared to other zoos or aquariums I've been to but it really wasn't an issue for us.  The food options were pretty limited, and of course expensive.  We got chicken fingers, pizzas, and ice cream, lots of fast food-like options for picky eaters but not a lot of healthy ones.  We had a great time, would definitely go back with just about anyone - especially if we had discount tickets :)

      The beacon of light in Shadyville. Yet, I had to make the trek out. Why? Because I'm a sucker for aquariums.

      Surprisingly, there are many unique marine animals in addition to your normal sharks, turtles, and walruses. The jellyfish exhibit was exhilarating. The seahorses were spectacular. Moreover, other oddities such as the flower hat jelly and the anableps can also be found within their respective confines.

      As for the 4-D IMAX experience, I must say that I want that 12 minutes of my life back. Worthless and an insult to one's intelligence.

      While smaller than, say, the Shedd, I did appreciate the fact that we didn't have to walk far to get from one exhibit to the next. And at $17 (including the IMAX), it tops my list of 10 things to see/do while in New York!
