      Review from Jeffrey K.

      Elite '11

      92friends

      296reviews

      Jeffrey K.

      Rockville Centre, NY

      12/2/2010 1 photo 1 Check-in Here

      Childtren's museum's are great -- it keeps the kids happy, you can let them run off and play, explore and touch all they want and they have fun exhibits.  This place is no exception.  For being in the heart of Brooklyn, this is a great place to take the kids on a rainy day during the summer as we did a few times this past summer.

      Yes, as others have noted, the parking is a bit hit or miss, but it's certainly not impossible, and we didn't have to feed meters or pay for a spot in a parking lot.

      The price for entry is reasonable (I forget exactly what it cost).  It is clean and spacious there are multi-cultural exhibits which reflect much of Brooklyn's neighborhoods, for the kids to explore and play in.

      I was a bit disappointed (but also sort of understood), that my son (age 8), could not join his sister (age 4), in younger children area -- the museum staff is apparently not permitted to allow children above age 5 or 6 into this special area.

      Also, on one of our visits, the "theatre" exhibit (where the kids can dance and put on a show along with other cultural dancers who are on screen), was not operational when we were there, but was working on another visit.

      I also see that some of the exhibits / areas are beginning to show their wear and may soon be in need of refurbishing / updating . . . but for a kids museum, I can also understand some of the wear and tear that might occur from so many little hands and feet.

      Also, the last time we were there, upstairs in the library, they had a snake exhibit where the kids got to pet the snake and ask the handler some questions.

      If you are in Brooklyn, and need to do something with the kids, this is absoluately a place for them to enjoy.

      So I get a call...It seems that my niece volunteered me to Chaperone a school trip to the Brooklyn Children's museum.  So on a friday, I had a date with a special girl and approximately 30 of her closest friends.  Of course, we had only the best transportation, the yellow bus!  As I sat and listened to children scream as they were being thrown into the air as the bus hit bumps, I watched their teacher masterfully begin a splendid song as if she was a world renown conductor.  The children, instantly forgot about their journeys through the air and began to accompany her in what could only be described as the sound one hopes to hear when one hears the essential elements of harmony, calm, and youthful joy.  

      When we arrived, the parents went to the counter to pay.  They took Amex.  I was paired with another male parent and three children.  We were assigned a wonderful tour guy who took us around a museum that was a microcosm of the businesses and staples that make brooklyn great.  The children were allowed to walkin into stores and subway cars that were built into the place to resemble actual businesses.  It was amazing.  There was also a rule.  You HAVE to touch.  Everything!  It was a great time.  There was even a sandbox.  There were fish.  There were bread stores and pizza shops.  It was great.  The guide discussed lessons and engaged the audience.  Afterward we ate.  There was a cafeteria.  There was also a gift shop.  I was rather impressed with the place in general!

      On the bus we went.  The singing continued and eventually we arrived at school.  I asked my niece if she wanted to leave with me.  She said..Uncle Jamel, duh, I have to go learn.  Then she blew passed me and walked into the classroom.  Another child looked at me and said.."bye Niyah's uncle"...my purpose was served...sigh.....Five Year olds...

      Review from Cristina S.

       

      4friends

      14reviews

      Cristina S.

      Ridgewood, NY

      5/1/2010

      The Brooklyn Children's Museum is a decent alternative for toddlers and elementary kids if you can drive there. (Although there used to be designated bus pick ups locations indluding Williamsburg, if I remember correctly.) It basically offers the same standard entertainments that indoor play areas have, except with some educational touches. As a learning experience, its not anything exceptional, but with toddlers what you can do is limited no matter how educational they try to bill it. If you bring toddlers to the water exhbit in Totally Tots bring extra clothes and sandals because even though they have plastic smocks they don't work completely. Also bring your own lunch if you can, otherwise be prepared to pay big bucks for run of the mill salads and hot dogs. Our bill for two toddlers and two adults came to $30. The tot area is closed off so your little one doesn't wander off but it gets VERY crowded after  11.  Your best bet for sanity is the week, when you'll meet mostly fashionable granola park slopers and assorted locals. (This is a prime location to spot trend setting baby gear and pick up snooty mom magazines.) If you are looking for free, the museum offers free weekend admissions in the spring. Otherwise admission is $7.50 per person not including kids under 1.

      The museum is midsize but can be tricky to navigate and as others noted not without dangers. Off the tot exhibit into the main area, the stairs can be exciting/ dangerous for your littles. The tunnel ramp has its own dangers as there is no railing next to the canals exhibit and kids can wade in. These stairs lead to a theatre and cultural Brooklyn neighborhoods exhibits. The dragon head off the oriental store is fun for pictures. And the pizza place is fun for pretend cooking. The sandbox/gardening exhibit  can get feisty as kids vie for spades so keep your eyes peeled. The garden outdoor area is more fun for older kids. Our tot enjoyed the water ecosystem exhibit with turtles and fish though and the  cooking class in the lower classrooms. The library was lost on them and most other people. The bathrooms were fairly efficient affairs. Hurrah for family bathrooms wide enough for strollers.
      An in-the-know thing: if you pick up any phone in the culture exhibits you can call other stores. This can be an exhausting, expensive entire day trip for parents but the kids will have fun.

      Review from Jim and Sandy M.

       

      0friends

      11reviews

      Jim and Sandy M.

      New Paltz, NY

      4/22/2010

      We give this museum 1 star not for the exhibits or its usefulness to kids, but for its safety factor.

      Yesterday, we took our daughter to this museum for the first time and she had a lot of fun with the displays.  But the day ended badly when my wife set down her digital camera for a moment, turned away, and it was stolen.

      The museum staff told us to check the lost and found, and to call back the next day as someone might have picked it up thinking it was lost.  But no one ever returned the camera despite that fact that there was a business card inside the camera case.

      So, a word to the wise: if you want to check this place out with your kids, go ahead and enjoy.  But be very CAREFUL, and remember that despite all the gentrification this area has gone through, it's still Brooklyn and it's still part of a big city, a place where some very nice people are forced to mingle with those of very low ethical character.
