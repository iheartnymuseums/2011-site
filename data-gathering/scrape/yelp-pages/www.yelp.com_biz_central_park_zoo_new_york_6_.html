      The Central Park Zoo needs a serious intervention.
      CPZ is the anorexic cousin that lives in an uber cool city and has all the advantages that you, it's cousin that lives in the South Bronx never had.
      Location is awesome amidst the famous park. Around the corner from the Met, a hop, skip and jump ( by NY standards. By other standards it's like another city) from the MOMA and the Museum of Natural History seems like it would have more allure and at least more residents.
      With as many tourists and locals that frequent the park you'd think this zoo gets more action. CPZ is like that guy that never gets laid but is always making sexual references and desperate to bang anything requires oxygen to survive.
      It costs about 12 bucks for adults and children under 3 are free.
      They have a snow leopard, a polar bear, two monkeys, a bunch of birds in the "rainforest" and some turtles, penguins and two swans.
      I think I've seen more bears at the Gay nude beach at Haulover.
      It was about 35 degrees outside and, to some, walking into the Tropical Rainforest habitat would seem appealing. To me, It was stifling. It felt like a hot Miami night wearing fur. We all had to remove our coats and I think I may have felt sweat behind my knees.
      We saw tons of birds and lemurs and fruit bats.
      Wow, driving down Old Cutler Rd, I can see a wider variety of critters and not just the ones splattered on the pavement.
      The penguins were in tanks that seemed filled with the remnants of the fish they had been fed. While they were animated and fun to watch the area wreaked of nasty fecal, fish matter mixed with ice. Not a cocktail I was looking forward to inhaling.
      The polar bear was sleeping and the snow leopard was nowhere to be found.
      Thank goodness we were just killing time awaiting the crossing of the NYC Marathons finish line by our Croatian friend.
      I wouldn't spend the 12 bucks for this Zoo, Instead I'd use the 12 dollars to pay for the subway and bus fare to the Bronx Zoo which is incredible.
      I do not condone sequestering animals for our entertainment but I understand that sometimes it may be necessary in order to help their endangerment and the education of mankind.
      Now if only we could cage some of the animals that inhabit S. Fla...

      The Central Park Zoo is perfect for families with young children that want to see some interesting animals, but don't want to be overwhelmed.

      I think Central Park is amazing and by extension, so is the Zoo.  It's amazing to me that in the middle of one of the largest and most dense urban locations in the world you have an amazing park that has lakes, ponds, rinks, pools and even a ZOO!!

      7/31/2009 3 photos

      No, it's not a world-class zoo, but it's not trying to be. It is a solid urban zoo that makes the most of its limited acreage. It is well-laid out, with a clear progression through each of the animal displays, and there is plenty of variety. For only $10, it's actually one of the more affordable attractions in this city and it's a great way to spend a couple of hours in the park.

      Highlights:
      - the plethora of colorful birds in the rainforest enclosure. There are so many of brightly colored, bizarre-looking birds, it is literally teeming with them. I also spotted the flying foxes hanging upside down on the ceiling as well as some well-camouflaged tortoises.
      - the lemurs. Primates are always great to watch.
      - watching the tufted puffins during feeding time
      - polar bears. huge and deadly yet so cute at the same time

      They do have a pretty nice Penguin exhibit. It's not the cleanest and it smells a little but I don't remember being as close to as many Penguins before which was cool. Really the only other animals worth seeing would be the Polar Bear, the Snow Leopards (they have 2), the Red Pandas (also 2), and the Lemurs. They do have a few other small animals and some small exhibits with snakes and such but that's pretty much it. The exhibits are pretty small which makes seeing the animal relatively close a good possibility but you've got to second guess how good of a life they must have in such a confined space.

      If you have the urge to hand feed a sheep in the petting zoo or want to check out one of the animals they have here and don't mind spending the money but do mind taking a subway ride up to the Bronx Zoo then this is a cool little place in Manhattan but just don't hype it up too much and it won't be too bad.
