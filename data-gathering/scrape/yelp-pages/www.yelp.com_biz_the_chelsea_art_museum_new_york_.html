      Vivian H.

      Brooklyn, NY

      12/12/2010

      I like Surrealists. I like post-Impressionists. I like the Old Masters. I never was much a fan of Abstract Expressionists...until now.

      This is one of the smaller museums in Manhattan, but much more charming than the daunting MoMA and Met combined. It is less crowded with three spacious floors of exhibits.  The second and third floors are permanent collections, with the third floor dedicated to Miotte.

      The first floor is currently exhibiting, "Abstraction Revisited." It is on display until 12/16/10. So you better hurry if you want to catch a glimpse of this exhibition. This is a description of the exhibit, taken from their website:

      "This show "Abstraction Revisited" examines the work of various contemporary artists engaged in abstraction, alongside examples from some of the first-generation masters of Abstract Expressionism. The works were chosen for their visual impact and the strong dialogue they establish between the first generation and today's abstractionists."

      The musuem is surrounded by  various Chelsea galleries, so after you finish here, you can check out all the other galleries in area. Makes for a great Saturday afternoon.

      They have a gift shop and a small coffee shop serving illy coffee.

      Jackson Pollock would have been proud of me.

      Review from Liz P.

      Liz P.

      New York, NY

      8/2/2010

      I'm not just giving them 4 stars because I volunteered here for a month or two, it really is a nice museum.  Not overwhelming like some, only 3 floors in this really nice modern, loft building.  Love love love the central glass stairs, and its the home of the Jean Miotte collection!  The place has history and puts together some really nice exhibits.  

      Cheap rate to get in, think it's like 8 bucks and even cheaper if your a student! $4?  Not too shabby! Small gift store and mini mini cafe area but they have Illy coffee!  Plus!  

      They host an event like once a month and those are always a lot of fun, got to attend 2 while I was there.  Place was always packed, young/hip crowds, excellent chance to mingle and meet new people.  Highly recommend trying to attend one of these events, but get there early, lines form!  

      I also recommend volunteering there, their not too demanding and fairly flexible.  I was there like 1 hour a week, met some people, got behind the scenes if you will and learned a lot about the pieces on show and the building itself.

      Listed in: Los Museos

      Geraldine K.

      New York, NY

      6/23/2010

      The two times I've come to the Chelsea Art Museum were for events, rather than to see the museum itself.  The first one was for an auto launch party and show, and the second was for a chamber music concert.  The first time, the event was housed in the lower part of the museum, where there was open bar - it was quite a nice venue space.  The second time was on the top floor.  In essence, I've gotten a holistic view of the museum at disparate times, and I can say that as a venue space, it's great!  Perhaps it's a bit too far out west, but in nice weather, the walk from the C/E trains isn't too bad.  However, I'm not too impressed with the art that's exhibited there.  Apart from the fact that it's nowhere close to Museum Mile, I'm sure the Chelsea Art Museum generates better and more revenue as a venue space more than anything else.  For art, it may be wise to defer to other cultural titans in the city.

    About This Business

    Provided by business

    Specialties

    Chelsea Art Museum presents contemporary thematic exhibitions complemented by public programs intended to foster critical thinking about today's world. Through its exhibitions and programs, CAM offers unique opportunities to gain insight into cutting-edge visual, new media, and performing arts, all within an inviting, intimate setting. The Chelsea Art Museum is founded on the legacy of the abstract expressionist painter, Jean Miotte, whose foundation is housed within the Museum. Through his painting, Miotte strove to build a bridge between cultures and transcend national boundaries to form a truly international artistic language. It is in this spirit that CAM presents its exhibitions and programming.

    CAM Facts & Figures
    * Founded as a 501 (c)(3) nonprofit charitable organization in 2002
    * Features 24,000 square feet of exhibition space spanning three floors
    * Welcomes approximately 100,000 visitors per year
