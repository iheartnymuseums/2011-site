      Christine H.

      Amesbury, MA

      5/1/2010 2 photos

      love this place. we wanted to go to Madame Tussauds but the line was out the door and practically around the corner.. so we went next door to Ripleys where we walked right in! (keep in mind we went on 12.30.09 - the day before new years in NYC!!)

      We are in college, so we got a student discount (you need your college ID with you) and paid right around $20 each. (Madame Tussauds is $35+ each!!) anyway, we paid without a hassle and walked in.

      the four of us spent about an hour and a half just looking around which is a really long time for us because we are pretty quick.. but there is just so much to look at. you can take pictures with all of the Ripleys features which is really cool - definitely bring your camera!!  At the end of the tour there is a mission-impossible laser type room that you can try to maneuver through. We are all mid-twenties and decided against that option (there were all kids in the line)... but also at the end of the museum there is this dark spinning tunnel that you can walk through - absolutely crazy. If you've been before, you know what we're talking about - if you haven't, you have to try it.

      Overall I would absolutely recommend this place to a friend, probably over the wax museum. It's a lot more interesting (Madame Tussauds is a lot of hype...), there is a TON of stuff to look at and it's well worth the admission. The only reason I didn't give it 5 stars was because of the crowds.. which I guess is unavoidable in Times Square.

      6/15/2010

      Went around 2:00 on a weekday afternoon, and there were NO crowds whatsoever. In fact, I did all I could to avoid the two kids behind me because I was savoring the quiet so much.

      A lot of interesting exhibits, and I had no idea that Ripley himself was such a well-traveled and fascinating person...if somewhat morbid in some of his collections.

      I liked the Black Hole room the most - it made me dizzy the first time I walked past, but since there was no one around, I kept walking back and forth until I got used to it.

      The cross-section of a human head was gross, and I thought it should be covered in a curtain or something for people who might not want to chance upon it. Yes, I'm squeamish when it comes to that sort of thing...and there was a whole room of x-rays and chainsaw-in-head graphics to tickle my brain. Wonderful.

      All in all though, the exhibit was smaller than I expected. I finished in less than an hour, and thought I had seen most things, from Abe Lincoln's hair to a headless chicken. The first few chambers are probably the best. After a while, it becomes more of a museum exhibit than a Believe iI or Not.

      Alright, I travel a lot & rarely take the time to visit things like Ripley's in my own back yard.  On impulse, and with friends visiting from out of town, I bit the bullet.

      I imagined a visit to be something like a dissappointing Coney Island side show, but I was remarkably surprised.  Under their marquee they had a man standing outside hammering a nail into his nose!  Sure it is a bit disturbing, but he got our attention.  Curiosity got the best of us & we ventured into the lobby where the aforementioned hammer-head gave us a discount coupon that helped us save quite a bit of money.  The staff was helpful in explaining what we'd see inside and there was no annoying line to wait in like the wax museum next door.  My friends made up their mind & we were in.

      Once we got inside, I was amazed to see just how many weird thing stuff they've got on display.  As a child, I'd visited a Ripley's somewhere on a boardwalk and this is infinitely better.  For starters, it's huge!  There were two floors and a bunch of different rooms that ranged from housing an electric chair and torture devices to the thing with the shark and a truly stunning jade sculpture that was just amazing.  Being a bit of a sports fan, I was surprised to see the uniform worn by Babe Ruth and to know that Ripley himself played major league ball right here in NYC.

      We took a bunch of pictures inside and I think the one of my head in a jar will grace my official Halloween party invite.  Would I go again - sure.  We spent about an hour and a half on this visit and there were still things I'd like to see again.  

      Walking to work each day in this city is an adventure, but our visit to Ripley's seemed to sum up everything that makes the Big Apple unique - nothing is unimaginable.

    About This Business

    Specialties

    Unlike any other attraction in the world, Ripley's Believe It or Not! Times Square features more than 500 real, fascinating artifacts displayed in over incredibly 20 themed galleries.  Visitors are guaranteed to see, touch, experience and witness things they'd never find at home.  Dozens of interactive elements keep kids engaged while curiosities of art, science, history and humanity impress even the most experienced explorer.  Featured artifacts range from the bizarre to the remarkable and include the world's largest display of shrunken heads, ancient fossils, living creatures to off-beat Americana and outrageous art.

    The Odditorium now features New York's only LaseRace - a dynamic challenge of skill, agility and timeliness against a high-tech maze of lasers.

    This is the largest Odditorium in North America and showcases items that can be seen no where else.

    History

    Established in 2007

    Opened in 2007, Ripley's Believe It or Not! Times Square is built on a 90 year tradition of sharing the world's most astonishing, unique and incredible finds with the public.  A museum-style presentation, the Times Square Odditorium is designed to surprise, inspire and intrigue visitors from all over the world and, yes, the locals too.

    Stephen's been with Ripley's since 2008.
