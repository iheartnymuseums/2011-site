      Review from Brian N.

       

      264friends

      100reviews

      Brian N.

      Montclair, NJ

      1/18/2011

      I went here last Friday with mi favorita to see the King Tut Exhibit. We were both excited to go. I had been to a similar exhibit at the Luxor (Las Vegas) way back in 1999.

      We paid for the Golden Ticket, which covers the tour, the audio accompaniment for the tour, and a 3-D movie. $40+/person. Free coat check (although it was limited and some people could not check their coats.)

      The tour was nice. You walk through a maze of different Egyptian artifacts in glass cases with printed descriptions for everything, and some you could punch in a number on your audio piece and listen to a more detailed description. At the end, you go to a room to see the 3D movie.

      There were some really interesting artifacts, a lot to see really, but the layout just seemed so uninspired, which I felt took away from the exhibit. I did like that you could walk right up on the glass cases for a really close look, and in most cases you could walk all the way around the subject for a good 360 degree view.

      I usually love this kind of thing but actually felt myself thinking, "is it over yet?"

      The 3-D movie was a waste really. Short, boring, I honestly can't even remember what they covered in it.

      I enjoyed the exhibit I experienced at the Luxor much more. This exhibit kinda felt like the bootleg version.

      This exhibit ended on Jan 17th. I was thinking about checking out their Pompeii exhibit which I believe is up next, but it's just a maybe after seeing this.

      Sarah L.

      8/3/2010

      We've been to both the King Tut exhibit and the Titanic one last year. You can easily find discounts online before purchasing your tickets. For King Tut, there was a TravelZoo discount of $10 off. However, once they added taxes, surcharges, and will call pickup (because we didn't have a printer in our hotel room), it amounted to $54.13. A tiny discount is better than no discount, though. Our discount limited our time to view the exhibit from 5-9pm, which was nice because it was cooler at that time, weather-wise, and there were fewer people about.

      They do not allow bags in the exhibit, so you will need to check them in. There are two bag check counters so remember which one you dropped off your purse. Walking through the exhibit will take about 1.5-2 hours, depending how crowded it is. There are audio guides provided but there are plenty of things to read. What I do appreciate about their exhibits is that the descriptions are printed multiple times and displayed at different heights, so you don't have to all crowd around one area and block everyone's view.

      The King Tut exhibit is a bit misleading in that while there are a few artifacts from his tomb, the majority of its contents are from people related to him. They kind of have it set up where you learn about the regime before his reign, how his tomb was found, and who was his father, etc. While I'm not surprised that there few items from his tomb, I was hoping for some more information about Howard Carter and the supposed mummy "curse".

      Last year's Titanic exhibit was more much thorough and interesting. There was a wealth of information and we must have spent over 2 hours poring over everything. I felt that "Titanic" was well worth the money. King Tut, not so much. It was good, but not great. I definitely would be mad if I had to pay full price for a ticket.

      I've been to three exhibits at the Discovery Times Square and they were all really good (Titanic, Leonardo DaVinci, and King Tut). King Tut is there now and I have to say, it was a really solid and good exhibition that I would recommend seeing.

      The museum itself is easy to get to. It's kind of expensive but if you search for a coupon code online, you can find a code for 10% off if you buy your tickets online (I can't recall the code, sorry). I would suggest buying the tickets online, just in case. It can get busy and it saves you time to wait in line once there. The tickets are for a timed entry but if you come earlier than your time and it's not too crowded they let you in.

      The exhibit itself had lots of artifacts, historical facts and it kept your attention. It wasn't overpowering or too much to read like some exhibits can be. It was really cool to see the video of Howard Carter bringing back the artifacts he discovered in the tomb and to see newspaper headlines announcing the discovery.

      Unfortunately the mummy itself is a replica but I completely understand why. It would be silly to transport that mummy anyplace and risk damaging it! It was still cool to see how it looks. I would recommend!!

      Titanic better than Da Vinci, but probably won't be back for King Tut

      Although both exhibits are officially over, you might never know which part of the country these exhibits will pop-up again, so I'm sure this review will be of some use. How do I know that? The King Tut exhibit was on display at deYoung museum in SF last year.

      DaVinci - I like how when you enter, they show you a short film about DaVinci, and then the screen slides away, reviewing all of the designs on Display. Unfortunately, the cool exhibits are few and far between. I like the mechanical lion, as that's the most complete (and to scale) design out of all of them.
