      I could not wish for better weather to attend this year's Apple Festival at the Queens County Farm Museum.

      The apple festival was really quite disappointing.  Apple picking usually means you get to pick them from the tree, not from a barrel!  Not only that, but there was nary an apple cider donut to be found.  WTF??!!!!!

      New York, NY

      4/20/2008

      This place ROCKS if you love nature, farms and all that.  ** TOP restaurant? ? ? ?  Read the review!

      1.  You can get there by busses (just transfer from one to another).

      2. If you do go by car, there is usually free parking in this huge parking lot down from the Farm.

      3.  If you go there on any Event day (i.e. Pow wow, County Fair day, or other events), get there two hours before the event begins if you want to find a quick parking spot in the  free parking lot.    No matter what time you do get there you will find parking in the lot or on the street, you just might have to walk further if you come later on.

      4. Events here are the most awesome events in the city.  It's great variety with no problem of parking.  The pow-wow was totally fantabulous.  The County Fair was a dream come true.

      ****
      *******THIS review below is about their COUNTY FAIR which is coming up this September: I've posted information abut the 2007 fair, and you can bet that 2008 will be just as great only much better.

      At the county fair  (2007) there were:

      1.  stilt-walkers, Uncle Sam
      2. log -rollers , lumberjacks contest
      3. 14 piece band and great singer
      4. smaller bands --Irish Music and one song from Pink Floyd
      5. Irish Dancers and Irish singers
      6. Carousel music from the genuine carousel organ (no merry-go-round, just the music).

      7. THE BEST corn anywhere!!  (I've had corn from the farm, from Suter's farm stand on Main street in Ohio, and from many other fairs.  This corn was the best, tastiest, freshest and no comparison to anyone else's.  High priced, yes, four dollars for one ear of corn, but well worth it.

      8. One politician and one speech (it was great!).

      9. Puppet show indoors

      10. farm animals walking around right near the kids.  They were very accessible, not behind large fences. (The cows and hogs were behind the fence, but small animals --chickens, peacock, roosters , were all walking around freely that day.

      11. Free items  (Jet blue stickers, calendars, free coloring books and other items throughout the fair. (That made up a little for the high priced foods).

      12.  Expensive -- Seemed like smaller items were four dollars each and more than that.    (Bringing a bunch of children? Pack picnic lunches, peanut butter sandwiches and snacks, raisens , fruits etc. and then buy some awesome fries there to share with everyone.

      13.  Soda was about $2.00 or $2.50 a drink but if you think early , you can get dollar soda from the machine that is right on the grounds.

      Here's a plan:  Feed the kids before leaving home, so breakfast is a done deal , bring lunch picnic, and enjoy the fair.  And after you can go home for a home-cooked meal or  eat out at Nathans or Burger King.

      Bring cameras! You'll have some awesome pictures of this day.

      Okay, so why would ANYONE rate the Queens County Farm museum as one of their top restaurants?  Here is why:

      1. First, aww, it is casual and what better to be in a restaurant than casual? Relax, wear your jeans and t-shirts, no one will be upset.

      2.  Ahh, The corn!!  I had to rate this as a top restaurant simply because I have never had any better corn -on the cob--anywhere in the United States of America.   At the Queens County Fair, they sell corn, fresh cooked, on a stick.  They boil the corn in huge vats bigger than children, and they butter it up as if butter were free.  Yes, the fee is steep.  About four dollars for one ear of corn.

      But, THIS corn is so worth the four dollars.  You have to go there and try this one. You will see why I love it.    The fair sells other food also. The elephants ears were great , another word for funnel cakes -- or similar.   The selection of food at the fair is awesome and just as expensive.  

      In COMPARISON?  I have tried corn in most of the states that I have visited, even trying corn right off the farm, from Suter's in Ohio.  Nothing beats this corn at the Queens County Fair at the Queens County Farm Museum.  Could the secret be in the cooking? Perhaps.  But whatever the secret, I vote this a huge TWENTY when it comes to the best corn in the USA.

      There were tons of other things at this fair, too numerous to mention.  But it's just one big old country fair and it was beautiful !!
