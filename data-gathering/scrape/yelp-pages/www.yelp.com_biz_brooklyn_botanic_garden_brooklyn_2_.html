      Michael M.

      Manhattan, NY

      2/2/2011

      If I could pick only one favorite spot in the city, Brooklyn Botanic Garden would be it hands down.

      There's some strange magic to this place. After stepping through the gate you really do get a sense that you've left all the stress of the city behind. The garden is tranquil. It's blissfully quiet (well, on weekdays at least). There are gorgeous landscapes all around no matter what time of year you go. It even smells pretty.

      Of course, the garden is at it's showiest between late March and early May, when their collection of magnolias and cherries burst into bloom. This is also when the garden is at its most crowded, so don't go expecting any of that aforementioned tranquility and quiet.

      But perhaps I'm an oddball, because my favorite time to visit the garden is in winter. They have a sizable collection of plants that provide winter interest - conifers, evergreen bamboo, witch hazels with spidery yellow blooms, delicate snowdrops and winter aconites, and crocuses that carpet the ground in blue and purple in early March. So when nature is looking a bit drab and dead in other places around the city, this is a great place to come for a mid-winter green fix. Plus, winter admission is free.

      Be warned, if you're a plant nerd like me, you will be losing a lot of money in their garden shop. They have pretty much anything a plant lover would want - interesting home and garden plants, tools, books, seeds, bulbs, organic fertilizers and pest control products, and cute tchotchkes like Victorian blown-glass plant misters. I make it a point not to come here with my credit card anymore.

      Annie B.

      Brooklyn, NY

      1/25/2011 9 photos

      This place is a GEM.  A wonderful place that I'm lucky enough to live close to.

      Even in the winter there are things to do here, but in the spring?  Dear GOD.  I literally fell in love with this place on a sunny day last April.  Women and babies were picnic-ing under the shade of the magnificent cherry blossom trees as the petals fell, people were reading books on the benches of other cherry blossom-covered walkways.  The hyacinths and lilacs and hydrangeas had the bees all atwitter.  Am I gushing?  Yes, I am and I don't care.  

      There are wonderful little specialized gardens like the smelling garden with fun, scented plants like spearmint and lavender.  The Shakespeare garden has plaques with the lines that cite the plant it's next to.  Indoors is a great collection of bonzai trees.  The Japanese pond is a wonderful place to relax and be thankful for life.

      This would be a great place to get married, and I know they do a lot, though I hear they rush people a bit.

      Bathrooms were very clean in some random building I entered and there's a little food area where I got a snack that satisfied both wallet and hunger.

      If it were possible to hug this place, I would.

      I don't know about your home town Botanical Garden but I really love this one! I have been here at least once a year and seen different trees and plants at its bloom, in season.
