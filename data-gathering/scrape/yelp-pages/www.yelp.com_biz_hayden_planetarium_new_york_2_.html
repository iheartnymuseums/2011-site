      I was kind of disappointed with the planetarium. I had visions of laying back and being transported into outer space, in total darkness, surrounded by bright, vibrant stars.

      Not quite. Regular movie theater style chairs arranged in a circle (I recommend sitting in the back, even though front row seats instinctively seem better, as it's hard to see the whole screen from the middle).       Review from Matt E.

      Elite '11

      176friends

      1092reviews

      Matt E.

      Brooklyn, NY

      2/14/2010

      The Hayden Planetarium is a very cool looking globe shaped Planetarium in the glass building (The Rose Center for Space and Earth) connected to the Natural History Museum. I went there yesterday for the Journey to the Stars show narrated by none other than Whoopi Goldberg. The show is about 30min long and costs $24 ($16 if you're still hanging onto your old college ID). I found that to be a little over priced but the show was very cool and extremely informative. The information in the show will absolutely blow your mind.

      Before the show you wait in a large dark room full of LCDs playing a pre show video about the sun and stars. It's interesting and I'm glad there was something to watch while we waited. The room soon filled up however and by the time the doors to the planetarium were about to open the room was so cramped with people standing up on each other I thought I was on the 7 train during rush hour. In hind sight I probably would have stood in the back of the room where the crowd was probably not as bad because there really isn't any bad seat in the house (just the seats next to people talking). I wished the seats reclined a bit more because you do have to bend your neck a bit to look up but since the show is only about 30min long you're probably not going to have a sore neck for long (if at all).

      All in all it's a cool Planetarium and I enjoyed the show.

      Review from Kathleen R.

       

      317friends

      214reviews

      Kathleen R.

      Bronx, NY

      7/20/2009 1 photo

      It doesn't take much to amuse me.  A simple formula of 3f + 4w + 1b* usually yields a good time had by all.  But recent events suggest that it's time to diversify the old review portfolio. I.e., Yelp included a snippet of one of my reviews in its weekly Metro newspaper ad. Right above my picture, it read: "Category: Drinks."  Color me typecast!

      So here's an explicitly non-alcoholic review.  We've all heard of or gone to a showing of "The Wizard of Oz" accompanied by Pink Floyd, right?  The SonicVision show is kinda like that, but the Galaxy stands in for Dorothy and a Moby mix replaces Pink Floyd.  So if you've been on the hunt for a place that lets you listen to U2 while you watch eyeballs bouncing around planets, your search is over!  "Flight of the Conchords" fans will also chuckle when Bowie comes on during another space scene ("This is Bowie to Bowie...").  As I've already said, this is a substance-free review, so I can't comment on how other variables may heighten the experience.  But you can probably guess.

      In addition to the sounds and sights, the chairs shake at times in sync with the soundtrack.  Unfortunately, I found this uncomfortable and jolting.  Also, if the show is going on above you, they should really make the seats recline a bit more.  My neck was feeling it by the end of the 40 minute screening.  And in all seriousness, I wouldn't recommend the show to anyone who is epileptic, drunk or sensitive to motion sickness.  Seizures and / or *reversals of fortune* are no one's idea of a good time.

      Regardless, this was definitely one of the most unique dates I've been on in awhile.  I should also mention that if you get the tix from Goldstar (one of those free newsletters with discount offers), they're  $7.50 a piece.  Which is great, because in addition to being all the rage this season, "cultured and cost-conscious" will really never go out of style.

      *Where f=friends, w=well-made cocktail and b=bartender with yummy accent

      Cosmic Collisions!!!  What can I say, I'm a bit of a nerd and sitting in the planetarium and seeing the show was the highlight of our visit to the Rose Center for Earth and Space....

      This was our first trip to the planetarium and it was one of the top destinations on our "list of must dos....".  And let me tell you, it did not disappoint.       Review from Jay L.

       

      3friends

      19reviews

      Jay L.

      Philadelphia, PA

      1/23/2009

      As an Astrophysics major, I must admit that I am a little (read: insanely) biased. I love this place. All throughout college and all throughout my life here, the planetarium has been my haven, my mecca, my home. From the amazing lecture series and presentations to Friday and Saturday nights' Sonicvision, the Hayden Planetarium is an amazing place.

      The director of the planetarium is Dr. Neil DeGrasse Tyson. You might recognize him from NOVA specials or his appearances on The Colbert Report. He definitely has taken it upon himself to make the planetarium an experience to be had, for kids and adults alike. There are amazing shows in the dome and an incredibly detailed exhibit on our solar system and our place in the universe.

      If you're in town for a few days, or if you've lived in New York your entire life, or somewhere in between, the Hayden Planetarium is a must-visit location. I promise you won't be disappointed.
