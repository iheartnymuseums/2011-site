      Review from zachary l.

       

      3friends

      zachary l.

      Brooklyn, NY

      11/16/2010

      Though this place could always use a bit of improvement in terms of the numbers of shows, I think Yeshiva University Museum's been getting way way WAY better over the last year and a half that I've been familiar with it.  Maybe if they'd go by their acronym, YUM, it'd fare even better.  

      I spent much of last year using the library in the building where the museum is based--the Center for Jewish History--and saw a number of fantastic, though poorly attended exhibitions.  They're a bit esoteric at times, but I for one like a museum that doesn't treat it's visitors like bunch of children with shit-filled diapers.  They had one exhibition on artists interpreting the story of creation from the first chapter of the bible.  Might sound dry, but it was honestly one of the best exhibitions I'd ever seen.  It was, though, a traveling show.  They have a show up now on the home movies of Polish immigrants to the US who went to visit their families back home  in the 1930s.  Really fantastic, home-grown show.  I've never seen anything from their permanent collection though, which I have no idea about.  With something like 763 Jewish museums in New York--give or take--I'd be surprised if more then a handful have a decent collection.  That said, on my few visits, I've found the exhibitions to indeed be quite interesting--though, of course, some more than others.

      In terms of the overall experience, yes, it's not the most comfortable place to enter.  Security is slightly less invasive then an El Al flight, but I've never felt violated in any way, and the people seem generally affable.  Also, I think these folks in the front work for the building and not for the museum.  

      I guess the measure of any museum is supposed to be its visitorship though.  For me, it's more about the museum experience, and part of that is NOT experiencing crazy crowds and especially annoying people.  Well, sadly for the museum, but good for the visitor, this place is usually pretty light on visitors.  That's great if you ask me--even if the guard follows me around here and there.  You can basically get the place to yourself.

      In terms of "throwing money away" as another reviewer put it, he probably would have done well to check the museum's website which lists several days with free admission, or part of days at least.  So, if he really wanted to see a show there, he would have been able to do so without spending a dime.
