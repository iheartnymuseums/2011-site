      I feel kind of bad saying this, but we were underwhelmed by the Eldridge Street synagogue.  We live on the Lower East Side and I greatly appreciate the strong Jewish history in this part of NYC, plus I followed the restoration efforts with tremendous interest.  However, I can't help but compare it to other restored synagogues I've visited (admittedly in other countries, primarily) and this one is much more understated.  Perhaps that was intentional - but from everything I'd read, I somehow expected a more special interior.

      Having said that, it was worth visiting (we were there on June 21) - and we took advantage of their free Monday tours (I think they're only free during summertime).  Our guide was very friendly and extremely enthusiastic (a little over the top in the latter regard, as the tour really lagged).

      I am not really a museum person, and don't usually go in for synagogue tourism, but this synagogue was breathtaking.  While beautiful on the outside, it is definitely worth a visit inside.  

      The tour was reasonable ($8 for students), and I enjoyed the tourguide's information, though if you are knowledgeable already about the Lower East side, it might seem really basic.  My friend is an expert, and he found this to be the case.   What I found to be most amazing was that the original floors, with the impressions made by original congregants' feet, have been preserved.  You literally feel the history at your feet.

      Also, a great little gift shop, where I found my grandmother's birthday present!!

      This is the most beautiful and oldest synagogue in the Lower East Side of Manhattan, home to waves of immigrants and the turn of the 19th Century.  This synagogue lay abandoned for decades as the local congregation dwindled in the 20's and 30's until it was re-discovered and restored, a project that continues to this day.  Truly a beautiful and historic place - it is on the National Register of Historic Places.  If you are at all interested in the history of US immigration or Jewish history it should not be missed.  (Also see the Tenement Museum which is nearby).

    About This Business

    Provided by business

    Specialties

    The Museum at Eldridge Street, based in the newly restored 1887 Eldridge Street Synagogue, is one of New York City's most inspiring sites. Located in a dynamic part of NYC, where Chinatown meets the old Jewish Lower East Side, we present the story of the Jewish immigrants who settled on the Lower East Side and draw parallels with the experience of diverse newcomers to America.

    History

    Established in 1986

    The Museum at Eldridge Street. founded in 1986 as the Eldridge Street Project, oversaw the restoration of the 1887 Eldridge Street Synagogue, a National Historic Landmark. The restoration was completed in December 2007 and the Museum is now opent to the public, offering tours, walking tours, concerts, lectures, festivals and other cultural events exploring immigrant history, architecture & historic preservation, and Jewish culture.

    Meet the Manager: Amy M.

    Amy is Deputy Director of the Museum.
