      Michelle L.

      New York, NY

      11/16/2010

      This is the largest collection of old and antique
      toy boats and toy soldiers you'll ever see.
      Malcolm Forbes and his sons were  kids at heart. Starting from
      childhood and continuing through their adult lives the Forbes family
      amassed a world-famous collection of epic proportions. When I say
      boats, I mean boats of every kind, shape, size, color, and
      material. When I say Toy Soldiers, it's so much
      more than that. Yes, there are toy soldiers from all over the world
      and you will also find the ice cream vendor on the parade grounds, the musicians' corps, the dogs hanging out, the medics, the tents, the trees, and even the food.  In addition,
      Don't miss  the enjoyable "game room" where you can see THE original Monopoly game when it was round and the inventor personally hand-wrote all the cards  Take the girls too!
      Please go ASAP-
      The Forbes Galleries will be closed Monday, November 22nd through
      Saturday, December 4th after which the Toy Boat, Toy Soldier, Monopoly
      and Trophy Galleries will close permanently.
      The last day to visit these collections is Saturday, November 20th.

      Review from Jessica F.

       

      97friends

      253reviews

      Jessica F.

      New York, NY

      6/6/2007

      The eggs are gone.

      That, to me, was the draw.  They're gone.  Apparently, the victims of settling the estate when Forbes died.  If you have that much money, do you really need to sell eggs the public got to see for nothing to the highest bidder?  Guess so.

      Without the eggs, there is:

      A collection of toy soldiers
      A collection of Monopoly games  
      A collection of boats

      Some other stuff.

      To be fair, it's a quirky place that 10-year-olds would find fun for a half hour, and is a fine place to kill time when you're waiting for a table to open up at Babbo or something.  After looking around and finding it not to my taste, I spent easily 30 minutes by the door, trying to get the piece of art to do its thing.  I don't want to tell you too much about it in case you go, because it is, while a little fleabitten and in need of some upkeep, the most charming thing about the trip.

      Damn you. Why did you have to sell off the eggs? Oh well. I did get to see them once before they went back to Moscow.

      Have no fear. There is still one very good reason to pop by this place: original Monopoly boards. I mean how can you beat that?

      The rest of the collection is rather mundane. Lots of toy boats and soldiers. However, the last time I took someone to show them the Monopoly boards, they had an outstanding collection of olympic medals and two torches! I thought it was very well done and great to see. Hopefully that sticks around for a while.

      This place is pretty damn close to Union Square, so why not take a half hour of your time and go check it out. I say it's worth it to see the boards (and now...the medals too).

      Review from Ryan H.

       

      24friends

      215reviews

      Ryan H.

      Albany, NY

      6/1/2005

      The late Malcolm Forbes Sr. really inches his way up my Favorite Billionaires list with this museum. I love seeing collections of people's stuff, and Malcolm was kind enough to leave his stuff in a free museum so that everyone could see it. He collected a lot of stuff that interested him, like a lot of people do. It just so happens that he collected Faberge eggs and not Coca-Cola or Elvis memorobilia like a normal person would. Is that so wrong? Besides the impressive egg collection, there's a gallery of antique toy boats, the oldest and original Monopoly game boards, and tiny toy soldiers re-enacting Revolutionary War battles and such. Like I said, a whole lot of junk. Did I mention it was free? It's a perfect place to spend an hour if you're in the neighborhood.
