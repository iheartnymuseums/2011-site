Staten Island Children's Museum
Established 1976
Location 1000 Richmond Terrace, Staten Island, New York, USA
Director Dina Rosenthal
Website Staten Island Children's Museum

The Staten Island Children's Museum is a children's museum on the grounds of Sailors' Snug Harbor[1] on Staten Island, New York, opened in 1976[2] following community and government support for the project.[3] The museum stresses a hands-on interactive approach to its exhibits [4] and currently offers visitors nine separate exhibit areas. Current exhibits include House About It (Home building), Bugs and other insects, Its a Dog's Life, Great Explorations, Sea of Boats, Ladder 11 and Big Games.[5]

In 2005, it was among 406 New York City arts and social service institutions to receive part of a $20 million grant from the Carnegie Corporation, which was made possible through a donation by New York City mayor Michael Bloomberg.[6][7]
